What Changed

The public story now matches the model.

The old homepage described `PCA-KMeans`, `15` identities, and season-by-season discovery. That is no longer the product. The current site is built around a stable taxonomy, a frozen scorer, and explicit identity explanations grounded in feature-level evidence.

Before

  • Exploratory framing Labels looked comparable across seasons even when the model was being re-formed within each cohort.
  • Identity inflation `15 identities` were marketed even though the public site did not match the implemented engine.

Now

  • Frozen reference taxonomy One reference window, one scorer, comparable outputs forward.
  • Primary + secondary fit Every team can be read as a main identity with explicit overlap into a second profile.

Why It Matters

Comparability is the point.

A taxonomy only becomes useful when the same label means the same thing across time and league context. That makes this site useful for scouting style clusters, league-level stylistic drift, and identifying where teams genuinely overlap versus where they only look similar in a single season snapshot.

Cross-league reading

See whether an MLS side shares more with a USLC or NWSL profile than with its nearest domestic rival.

Borderline teams stay interpretable

Near-split teams are not dumped into “unclear.” They are shown with a primary identity and a meaningful secondary pull.

Evidence beats vibe

Every assignment can be explained with feature-level separation rather than just a label pasted on top.
