The 15 Tactical Identities

The TVMS clustering engine evaluates teams across a comprehensive 15-metric matrix. When a team exhibits statistically elite variance (≥ 0.5 standard deviations above the mean) in a specific metric, they are classified under the corresponding identity.

Attacking Juggernauts

Metric: Expected Goals For (xG)

Teams generating overwhelming shot quality and volume, regardless of possession style.

High-Press Hounds

Metric: Opponent Directness

Chaotic pressing teams that force opponents into long, direct clearances rather than short build-up passing.

Vertical Threats

Metric: Attacking Directness

Transitional units that bypass the midfield with rapid, vertical ball progression into the final third.

Open Defenses

Metric: Expected Goals Against (xGA)

Systems exhibiting severe structural vulnerability or transitional defensive liabilities.

The Architects

Metric: Possession Volume

Methodical build-up teams that dictate the pace and shape of the game entirely with the ball.

The Disruptors

Metric: Defensive Disruption (g+)

Midfield destroyers who aggressively break up plays, intercept passes, and force turnovers in the middle third.

The Metronomes

Metric: Passing Impact (g+)

Teams that generate extreme offensive value specifically through line-breaking and high-leverage passing.

Progressive Carriers

Metric: Dribbling Impact (g+)

Sides built around dynamic ball-carriers who generate attacking value through take-ons and progressive runs.

The Gunslingers

Metric: Shooting Impact (g+)

Exceptionally clinical finishing teams whose value comes from converting difficult chances well above expected rates.

Box Infiltrators

Metric: Receiving Impact (g+)

Teams defined by elite off-ball movement, consistently finding space in dangerous, high-value receiving zones.

Air Superiority

Metric: Claiming Impact (g+)

Dominant aerial teams that neutralize opposition crosses and set-pieces through elite box command.

Volume Shooters

Metric: Shots For

Systems designed to simply bombard the opponent's goal with attempts, prioritizing quantity over perfect shot locations.

Siege Defenders

Metric: Shots Against

Low-block teams that willingly concede massive shot volumes, betting on shot-blocking and poor opposition finishing.

Tiki-Taka Purists

Metric: Possession Quality (%)

Systems that prioritize retaining the ball at all costs, resulting in elite pass completion percentages.

Passive Observers

Metric: Opponent Pass Quality (%)

Defensive shapes that apply zero pressure to the ball, allowing opponents historically high completion rates.

Balanced Systems

Metric: None (Below Threshold)

Teams that lack a single extreme statistical outlier. They play a versatile, balanced game without leaning heavily into any specific tactical ideology.
